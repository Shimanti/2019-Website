Recipe: Simple Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb linguine
  • 1 lb grape tomatoes, halved
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced (reduce crying by cutting and adding last)
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed or thinly sliced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 2 tsp dried basil
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 cup water
  • Freshly grated parmesan cheese
  • Etxra olive oil, salt and pepper

Instructions:

Although I love quick and simple dump-in-a-pot recipes, if you are willing to sacrifice a few extra minutes, then you might prefer the following recipe to the dump-in-a-pot version.

Cook the pasta as per usual, in a big pot. Saute the onions before adding garlic and inhale the smell a little. Then add the tomatoes. The result is a sweeter and tangier taste to the sauce. I have to admit, this one is my favorite.

  1. Boil pasta in pot, until al dente, about 8 minutes.
  2. Saute onion in olive oil until slightly brown, in a large skillet.
  3. Add garlic, red pepper flakes and stir until you smell the garlic.
  4. Add tomatoes, dried basil, salt, pepper, 1 cup water (or stock) and stir. Boil for 5 minutes.
  5. Mix tomatoes and onion sauce with pasta.
  6. Serve in bowls. Add (at least 1 tsp) parmesan to taste.
  7. Add olive oil, freshly ground salt and pepper to taste.
